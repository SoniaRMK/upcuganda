Values, Aims and Objectives

b

UNITY

To build the Republic of Uganda as one country with one people, one national Parliament and one national Government.

SOVEREIGNITY

To defend the independence and sovereignty of the Republic of Uganda and maintain peace and tranquility and to defend and preserve the Constitution of Uganda

DEMOCRACY

To organise the Party in such a way as to enable the people to participate in framing the destiny of our country.

SOCIAL AND POLITICAL DEVELOPMENT

To fight relentlessly against poverty, ignorance, disease, neo-colonialism, imperialism and oppression of any nature.

EQUALITY AND EQUITY

To fight for social justice, eliminate inequalities and ensure that no citizen of Uganda will enjoy any special privileges, status or title by virtue of birth, descent or heredity unless such privileges, status or title are entrenched in the Constitution of Uganda.

ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION

To protect the environment and promote environmentally sustainable development.

UPC

The roots of Uganda Peoples Congress (UPC) can be traced to the Uganda National Congress (UNC), which was formed in 1952 under the leadership of Ignatius Musazi the UNC emerged out of Uganda African farmers Union (UAFU) which was a movement of small holder farmers agitated for better prices of coffee and cotton. At the time, the main economic and political activities were concentrated in Buganda and naturally the leadership of the day. With time, the Baganda lost out on the political leadership because they lacked the local base and support of Mengo established. This, therefore, changed the ethnic and character of the leadership of the Party. The Party was the first Uganda Party to be established and to have branches country wide.

In terms of ideological outlook, UNC was generally socialist in orientation. There was reason why UNC adopted a socialistic stance. In order to be able to negotiate for better prices the UNC leadership sought the assistance of Fener Brokway sent John Stone house, another socialist to help in organizing both farmers' cooperative movement association and UNC itself. Stonehouse was also able to arrange for Ignatius Musazi to take time off, both Soviet Union for assistance basically because they were fighting colonialism which was perpetrated by the western world. It was in the vain that UNC established an office in Cairo under John Kalekezi as a communication and transit center, since that time, Uganda has maintained close links with Egypt.

Our Objectives

Good Governance and Ethics

To relentlessly spearhead efficiency, transparency and accountability in governance and fight all forms of corruption.

Political Power

To compete for and acquire political power at all levels of government.

Rights of the Child

To support and promote the struggle for the rights of children.

2.12 Rights of Persons with Disabilities.


Conflict Resolution and World Peace

To support and advance the cause of national and international conflict resolution, development, disarmament and world peace.

Gender Issues

    To support the cause of women’s emancipation and advancement by mainstreaming and integrating women and women issues effectively into the body fabric of the Party and Party leadership;

     To support and advance gender equity and equality in the Party programmes, structures, decision making process, economic and social activities;

     To relentlessly advocate for the rights and cause of women nationally and internationally.

On Interference with Rights and Freedom of Others

To ensure that in the enjoyment of one’s rights and freedoms, no person shall be allowed to interfere with or prejudice the rights and freedoms of others.

Co-operation and Relations

To support and affiliate with organisations, whether international or otherwise, whose values, aims, objectives and aspirations are consistent with those of the Party.
