UPC ROOTS

01.

Uganda Congress Peoples (UPC) has always indicated in a number of its documents that it is a social democratic party.

The roots of Uganda Peoples Congress (UPC) can be traced to the Uganda National Congress (UNC), which was formed in 1952 under the leadership of Ignatius Musazi the UNC emerged out of Uganda African farmers Union (UAFU) which was a movement of small holder farmers agitated for better prices of coffee and cotton. At the time, the main economic and political activities were concentrated in Buganda and naturally the leadership of the day. With time, the Baganda lost out on the political leadership because they lacked the local base and support of Mengo established. This, therefore, changed the ethnic and character of the leadership of the Party. The Party was the first Uganda Party to be established and to have branches country wide.

02.

In terms of ideological outlook, UNC was generally socialist in orientation. There was reason why UNC adopted a socialistic stance. In order to be able to negotiate for better prices the UNC leadership sought the assistance of Fener Brokway sent John Stone house, another socialist to help in organizing both farmers' cooperative movement association and UNC itself. Stonehouse was also able to arrange for Ignatius Musazi to take time off, both Soviet Union for assistance basically because they were fighting colonialism which was perpetrated by the western world. It was in the vain that UNC established an office in Cairo under John Kalekezi as a communication and transit center, since that time, Uganda has maintained close links with Egypt.

the fact that UNC adopted a socialistic orientation meant that it had an uphill task dealing with the colonial administration and religious establishment. The result of this was that the party was consumed by internal conflict fermented by the colonial rulers. very many small parties were encouraged to form like the progressive Party under E.M.K. Mulira, in January 1955. The Progressive Party never achieved much of a following. United Congress Party (UCP) was formed by a splinter group in 1956 under Dr. E. Muwazi but was unable to capture the branches of UNC. UCP decided to take the line of Buganda Government to fight direct elections. In 1958, political leaders in the LEGCO particularly those from outside Buganda formed Uganda Peoples Union (UPU) under the leadership of W.W. Rwetsiba. The Uganda Peoples Union represented the first major effort at political organization lead from outside Buganda. the Democratic Party, which was formed in 1956, party as a bulwark against communism and marginalization of Catholics in Buganda, was in 1956 led by 41 votes to Kintu with 42 votes

03.

Major political developments took place in the country the recommendations of the wild constitutional committee, appointed after the Partial General Election of 1958. the 1958 elections were partial because they did not cover the whole country. The colonial administration gave Districts and Kingdom Administration as option whether to have direct or indirect representation in the reformed and expanded Legislative council. Ankole and Bugishu chose indirect elections while Buganda Kingdom refused to participate in the entire election process. The Kabaka's government claimed that the appointment of the Speaker to replace the governor as the presiding officer in the LEGCO was a major departure from the Hancock arrangement of 1955, which arrangement enabled the Sekabaka Mutesa to return from deportation.

Karamoja at the time was considered a special district was not invited to participate in these elections. The governor continued to present their interests of the districts in LEGCO. The reason for this serious divergent of policies and actions where many, whose consequences were bound to be cumulative and brave. Between 1956 and 1960 UNC was charactered by internal conflicts and splits which calumniated into the emergency of A.M. Obote as a compromise candidate in one of the factions to replace I.K. Musaki. The change took place at the Annual Delegates Congress held in Mbale on the 12th of January 1959. On 9thMarch 1960, UNC Obote faction merged with UPU to form UPC.  In June 1960, a moderate group of Buganda launched the United National Party (UNP) under the leadership of Apollo Kironde the former member of UNC and Y.K Lule, a Lecturer at Makerere University and Abu Mayanja as its publicity secretary. This signed the death warrant of UNC. UNP soon afterwards decided to be absorbed by UPC.


Social economic policy                                                                                                                    

The economic policy platform of UPC has always been based on the Party's political and economic ideology. Its ideology is molded by the Party's struggle and history of country. UPC believes in serving the masses and its policies have always been motivated by consideration of the wishes of the people in consonance with the principals of democracy and social justice. At the Delegates Conference of the Party held in 1969, UPC promulgated the common's Charter. This was the first Ideological statement marking the attempt by the party to set out a socialist agenda for Uganda. In the " first step to move to the left" the Party:

i.        Marked the beginning of a new political culture and new way of life, whereby the people of Uganda as a whole - their welfare and their voice in the national government and in their local authorities where paramount.

ii.      UPC declared to be anti-capitalists and anti-federalists

iii.    UPC denounced tribalism, colonialism and privileged class

iv.    In the Nakivubo pronouncement, UPC as a party of its move to the left announced partial nationalization of some private properties.

the move to the left caused a great concern to the western powers. they could not understand or tolerate the pronouncements, partly as a result of the move to the left UPC government was overthrown on the 25th of January 1971, which was largely engineered by the western powers using Israel as a proxy.

                                                                                        

 

Achievement of the 1960's.

In spite of various challenges of 1960, the UPC government was able to publish and successfully implement two-five year development plan and was able to:

i.        Build and equip 23 rural hospitals throughout Uganda and man them professionally. These hospitals included Abim, Anaka, Apac, Atutur, Bududa, Bugiri, iganga, Itojo, Kagadi, Kambuga, kapcorwa, Moyo, Kawolo, Kiboga, Kiryandongo, Kitagata, Pallisa, Gombe, Nakaseke, Yumbe, Bundibugyo, busolwe and Nebbi.

ii.      Tarmac thousand miles of road.

iii.    Build and equip many primary schools, secondary schools and technical institutes.

iv.     Extend railways services to the western and northern Uganda.

v.       Expand district farm institutes and establish effective agricultural extension services.

vi.     Develop a tourism industry with a number of hotels under the Uganda Hotel projects. Built hotels in many places.

vii.   Improve the cooperative movement to the extent of establishing its own bank- cooperative bank.

viii. The Universal primary education (UPE) which has been terribly mismanaged by the highly corrupt NRM government was conceived and formulated by the UPC in 1969 Annual Delegates Conference. it was passed by the parliament as one of the projects of the 5 years plan 1966 to be operationalize in 1970s in phases.

ix.    Transform a small Uganda credit savings society into giant peoples own bank; Uganda Commercial bank.

x.      Start television broadcasting and expand radio broadcasting. Africanize and expand the civil service.

xi.    negotiate and agree on the East African treaty which ushered in the East African Community.

xii.  strengthen and expand UDC including UGADEV, the forerunner of Uganda Development bank

xiii.Establish the bank of Uganda

xiv. Establish the diary cooperation

xv.   establish ranches and Kabimba rice scheme.

Between 1971 and 1979 the leader and many members of UPC took refuge in Tanzania and Zimbabwe. The Party is very grateful for the hospitality and assistance extended to party leader and members during those difficult times. The struggle against the military dictator Idi Amin which was spearheaded by the Party was greatly assisted by the governments and citizens of these countries.

Mengo establishment was not comfortable with the political development in the country. they threatened to declare Unilateral Independence (UDI) in December 1960as the country was preparing for political change. it was the political maturity and vision of the leadership of UPC (during the Lanchester House Constitutional Conference) that helped to avert UDI and brought back Buganda into fold. the efforts of UPC to bring back Buganda to remain part of Uganda was not motivated by greed for power, rather by UPC's commitment to negotiate and dialogue leading to national unity and stability. This was quickly followed by the need to define the relationship between the central government, the kingdoms, Busoga territory and other district administrations in the rest of the country. A commission under the Chairmanship of lord Munter helped to define these relationships.

the colonial administration held a general election in March, 1961 for the Legislative council (LEGCO) in spite of Buganda's refusal of direct elections.  the Mengo establishment boycotted these elections. these elections were won by DP whose base was Buganda and field candidates in all constituencies throughout the kingdom. the Democratic Party won with 43 seats 407,461 votes and UPC received 35 seats and 488,334 votes. in the same year, Uganda was granted internal self-government and the DP leader Bendicto Kiwanuka was named Chief Minister an leader of Buganda Business. Later that year a Lanchester House constitutional Conference was held in Britain attended by all political stakeholder to determine the political future of the country. this pre independence conference decided that a new mandate though holding a general election should be sort before Britain relinquished her responsibilities and protection" among other decisions. Accordingly, another general election was held in April 1962, these elections were for the areas outside Buganda, Buganda had been allocated 21 seats, which were to be indirectly filled by the Lukiiko as decided at the pre-independence Lanchester house conference. All the 21 seats went to Kabaka Yekka (KY). The UPC won 37 seats with 494,959 votes and in alliance with Kabaka Yekka formed the majority in the National Assembly and hence formed the Government. The Democratic Party received 24 seats with 415,718 votes.

Early Challenges:

The first challenge of UPC Government at the independence was how best to involve the majority of the population into meaningful and beneficial economic activities without raising racial animosity and hostility. the immigrant Asian minority issue was delegate and could have led to racial and emotional overtones. the Asians dominated the country’s trade and commerce and formed a strong cadre of middle technicians in the industry and public service. At the end of 1959 the breakdown of the entire civil service composed s follow; Africans 464, Europeans 667, Asians 488.

The other most politically explosive challenge of post-independence was the implication of the constitutional provision which required a referendum to be held within 2 years of independence to determine the wishes of the people of Bugangaizi , Singo, Buyaga and Buhekula and Buyanga whether they wanted.

i.        To stay under the jurisdiction of Buganda Kingdom or

ii.      Transfer their allegiances to Bunyoro Kitara kingdom or

iii.    Form a separate district.

The lost counties issues was a test for all stakeholders, whether or not Independent Uganda was to be governed under the rule of Law. as the government of the day UPC imbued with the democratic principles had no alternatives, but to abid by the provision of the constitution. The KY which was the partner of UPC apparently had hoped that somehow UPC government would find a way of not fulfilling this constitutional obligation. however, the referendum was run according to the rules that were set in the constitution and the people of Bugangayizi and buyaga elected to join the Buyoro Kitala kingdom while those of Singo, and Buwekula decided to remain as part of Buganda kingdom.

the next challenge was that Sekabaka Mutesa II who opted to be the president of Uganda and found himself in a dilemma when presented with the Lost’s status for signature. The constitution did allow for the signing of the status by the executive prime minister in case the president for one reason or another was not able to sign such status at a given time. Accordingly, Prime Minister Milton Obote signed both referendums to be held and affirm the results, this led to an internal uprising in Mengo leader to ouster of Katikiro Kintu basically as an escape goat. Micheal Kintu was succeeded by Joash Mayanja Nkagi then the minister of industry in the central government.

The 1966 political crisis participated by Daudi Ochieng’s parliamentary motion on 4th February, the arrest and detention of ministers on the 22nd February, and the May Lukiiko resolution demanding the removal of the central government from Buganda soil all of which threatened the peace and tranquility of the country. The May 166, Lukiiko resolution was reminiscent of the 1960UDO. The government of Uganda viewed it as a confrontation and treasonable. Therefore, the government had to act to preserve the unity and territorial integrity of the country. The issuing of confrontation between the central government and Mengo establishment resulted in the loss of life and flight of Sekabaka Mutesa II into self-exile. this was a very sad episode in the history of Buganda. In order to contain the situation, the UPC government put in place the interim constitution known as pigeonhole constitution. After relative positive stabilization, a draft constitution was presented to the Nation Assembly, upon which the National Assembly dissolved itself into a constituency assembly to debate the draft. the new republican constitution was promulgated in 1967.
